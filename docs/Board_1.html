When a partnership establishes a major suit fit and possesses 28 or more combined High Card Points (HCP), the opener must ensure the auction stays alive for slam investigation, even after a limiting response (like North's 3S). If opener holds 19+ HCP and partner shows 9-12 HCP and good support, opener should assume the potential for slam. The principle: Do not accept game immediately if your combined strength reaches slam levels. Instead, use a forcing bid, like a Cue Bid (e.g., bidding 4C or 4D to show first-round control in that suit) or 4NT (Key Card Blackwood), to find out if the hands complement each other for 12 or 13 tricks.
